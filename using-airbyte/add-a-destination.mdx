---
title: Add and manage destinations
description: Connect to a data warehouse, lake, or database where Airbyte will write your synced data.
---

A destination is the data warehouse, data lake, database, or other system to which you sync data. Adding a destination is the next step after you [add a source](/using-airbyte/add-a-source).

## Add a destination connector

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Destinations page">
    In the left navigation, click **Destinations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new destination">
    Click **New Destination**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find your connector">
    Search for or browse the connector you want to add.

    <Tip>
    Not sure which destination to use? Click the **Marketplace** tab and select **End-to-End Testing (/dev/null)** to simulate syncs without writing data anywhere. If you're on Airbyte Cloud, **Google Sheets** is easy to authenticate with and makes a great first destination.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the connector">
    Every connector has different options, but you typically provide authentication information and specify where to write data.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Airbyte Cloud (AI-assisted setup)">
        If you use Airbyte Cloud, you can set up connectors with the Connector Setup Assistant. The AI asks you questions, provides context, and guides you through each field. See [Set up connectors with AI](#set-up-connectors-with-ai) for details.
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Self-managed or manual setup">
        If you use a self-managed version of Airbyte, or if you prefer the traditional setup experience, fill out the setup form and click **Set up destination**.

        Airbyte tests the connection. Once the test completes, Airbyte takes you to that connector's Connections page.
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Modify a destination connector

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Destinations page">
    In the left navigation, click **Destinations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the destination">
    Find and click the destination connector you want to modify.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the configuration">
    Edit the fields in the form on the left side of your screen. Use the documentation panel on the right for reference.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Test and save**. Airbyte tests the destination to confirm the connection still works.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Delete a destination connector

<Warning>
Deleting a destination connector also deletes any connections that rely on it. Data already in your destination is not affected. However, if you recreate the connection later, a full re-sync is required.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Destinations page">
    In the left navigation, click **Destinations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the destination">
    Find and click the destination connector you want to delete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete the destination">
    Click **Delete this destination**, type the connector name in the confirmation dialog, then click **Delete**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Set up connectors with AI

On Airbyte Cloud, the Connector Setup Assistant uses AI to walk you through connector configuration. Instead of filling out a form, you have a guided conversation where the AI asks for the information it needs and explains what each field does.

This feature is in beta and may not be available for every connector.

## Reuse destination connectors

Connectors are reusable. In most cases, you only need to set up a destination connector once and can use it in as many connections as you need.

## Other ways to manage destinations

You can also manage destination connectors programmatically:

- [Airbyte API](https://reference.airbyte.com/reference/createdestination)
- [Terraform](/developers/terraform-provider)

<Tip>
When using the API or Terraform, you can speed up configuration by filling in values through the UI first, then clicking **Copy JSON**. This copies your configuration as a JSON string you can paste directly into your code.
</Tip>
