Airbyte connectors are grouped into four support levels. The support level determines who maintains the connector, how production-ready it is, and what kind of support you can expect when something goes wrong.

## Support level comparison

|  | Airbyte | Enterprise | Marketplace | Custom |
|--|---------|------------|-------------|--------|
| **Maintained by** | Airbyte | Airbyte | Community | You |
| **Production readiness** | Guaranteed | Guaranteed | No SLAs | Not guaranteed |
| **Support: Cloud** | Supported* | Supported* | No support | Supported** |
| **Support: Powered by Airbyte** | Supported* | Supported* | No support | Supported** |
| **Support: Self-Managed Enterprise** | Supported* | Supported* | No support | Supported** |
| **Support: Open Source (Core)** | Slack only | N/A | No support | Slack only |
| **Who can use them** | Everyone | Extra license required | Everyone | You only |

\*For Airbyte connectors, official support SLAs are only available to customers with Premium Support included in their contract. Otherwise, Airbyte addresses issues through the support portal as soon as possible.

\*\*For custom connectors, official support SLAs are only available to customers with Premium Support. Support is provided with best efforts — maintenance and upgrades are owned by the customer.

## Airbyte connectors

**Airbyte** connectors are built and maintained by Airbyte to a high quality standard. These are the connectors you'll find in the core catalog and they cover the most widely used data sources and destinations.

Airbyte connectors:

- Are tested, vetted, and production-ready.
- Are officially supported and available to all users.
- Experience few breaking changes. When an upgrade is required, Airbyte provides an adequate upgrade window and advance notice.
- Include certified database connectors like PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB, and Snowflake, along with popular SaaS connectors like Salesforce, HubSpot, and Stripe.

<Note>
If you're building a new data pipeline for production use, start with Airbyte connectors whenever possible.
</Note>

## Enterprise connectors

**Enterprise** connectors are premium connectors available exclusively for Self-Managed Enterprise and Airbyte Pro customers at an additional cost.

Enterprise connectors:

- Are built and maintained by the Airbyte team.
- Provide enhanced capabilities and support for critical enterprise systems.
- Are **not** available to Open Source (Core) or standard Cloud customers.
- Support larger data sets, parallelism for faster data transfers, and are covered under Airbyte's support SLAs.

Contact your Airbyte account team to learn which connectors are available at the Enterprise tier and to add them to your contract.

## Marketplace connectors

**Marketplace** connectors are maintained by Airbyte's community members using the [Connector Builder](https://docs.airbyte.com/connector-development/connector-builder-ui/overview). These connectors cover a wide range of data sources that are not yet in the core catalog.

Marketplace connectors:

- Are **not** maintained by Airbyte.
- Do **not** have support SLAs.
- Should be used with caution in production environments.
- May not be feature complete and may experience backward-incompatible, breaking changes without notice.
- Are available to everyone.
- Can be improved by contributors — [learn how to contribute](https://github.com/airbytehq/airbyte/blob/master/CONTRIBUTING.md).

In the Airbyte UI, Marketplace connectors display their popularity score and success rate data so you can make an informed decision before using them in production.

<Warning>
Do not rely on Marketplace connectors for critical production pipelines without thorough testing and a plan to handle breaking changes.
</Warning>

## Custom connectors

**Custom** connectors are connectors you build and maintain yourself, exclusively for your own workspace. You take full ownership of the connector's quality, reliability, and production readiness.

Custom connectors:

- Are built using the [Connector Builder](https://docs.airbyte.com/connector-development/connector-builder-ui/overview) (recommended for HTTP APIs), the Python CDK, the Java CDK, or any other language via Docker.
- Are visible only in the workspace where you created them.
- Are your responsibility to maintain and upgrade.
- Can receive official support SLAs only if you have Premium Support in your contract, and only with best-efforts coverage.

See the [Custom Connectors](/connectors/custom-connectors) page to get started building your own connector.

## Archived connectors

From time to time, Airbyte removes a connector from the active catalog. This typically happens when a connector has very low usage and is not actively maintained by the community. Archiving ensures the connector catalog maintains a minimum quality level.

Archived connectors:

- No longer receive updates or support from Airbyte.
- Remain source-available in the [`airbytehq/connector-archive`](https://github.com/airbytehq/connector-archive) repository on GitHub.

To take over maintenance of an archived connector, open a [GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/airbytehq/airbyte/discussions/). The connector must pass [Acceptance Tests](/build-connectors/testing-connectors) before you can start the un-archiving process.
